Culture Drop

Hello readers!

Welcome to Issue 42. We're dedicating the entire Deep Dive this week to the evolution of Afrobeats. As the genre continues to dominate global charts, we wanted to look backwards to understand exactly how we got here—and what might be lost in translation as the music travels.

Plus, our usual curation of the week's best reads, exhibitions, and sounds. Let's get into it.

The Deep Dive

There is a prevailing narrative in Western music journalism that treats Afrobeats as a spontaneous eruption—a sudden, joyful noise that inexplicably conquered Spotify playlists overnight. But the global afterlife of Afrobeat (the parent genre) and the explosion of Afrobeats (its pop descendant) is the result of decades of deliberate cross-pollination.

To understand the current moment, you have to look at the diaspora corridors: the Lagos-to-London flight paths, the early 2000s club promoters in Peckham, and the digital infrastructure built by independent African distributors when major labels weren't paying attention.
