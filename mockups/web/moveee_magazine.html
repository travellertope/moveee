Afrobeats at the Crossroads: A Conversation with Adekunle Gold

On globalisation, authenticity, and what it means to make music that travels. The Yoruba pop architect has stopped explaining himself to anyone.

Words by Fola Olawale
Published June 9, 2026
Reading time 12 minutes

In this piece

  • 01 On the Label Deal That Fell Through
  • 02 What Globalisation Takes From You
  • 03 What Comes Next
WRITER Fola Olawale
LOCATION Lagos, Nigeria
SECTION Interviews
SERIES Culture Economy
INDUSTRY Music & Ents

There's a particular brand of artistic confidence that Adekunle Gold carries into every room. At 34, the Yoruba pop architect has stopped explaining himself to anyone. His evolution from highlife-infused sweetheart to an unapologetic rockstar is a masterclass in sonic pivoting.

He's speaking to me from his Lagos studio, and I can hear the city in the background — motorcycle engines, hawkers, the faint hum of a generator. It's an auditory tapestry that anchors his latest project.

When I ask about the international label deal that fell through in 2023, he doesn't flinch. He leans back in his chair, taking a slow sip from a dark mug. "They wanted to erase the Lagos in my voice," he says finally. "I said no. Some deals aren't worth making. The industry here is..."
