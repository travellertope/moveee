Fela Anikulapo-Kuti

📍 Lagos, Nigeria
Live Music Music Production Ideas Visual Art

Fela Anikulapo-Kuti was a Nigerian musician, bandleader, composer, political activist, and Pan-Africanist. He is universally recognised as the pioneer of Afrobeat.

Combining traditional Yoruba percussion with American funk and jazz, Kuti's music was a revolutionary force in post-colonial Africa. His sprawling compositions often lasted over 15 minutes, serving as vehicles for blistering critiques of military dictatorship, corruption, and imperialism.

FOOD

Jollof Rice

Food & Drink Street Food Ideas

A classic West African rice dish made with a base of tomatoes, onions, scotch bonnets, and regional spices. It is a staple at celebrations and the subject of fierce regional rivalries.

The exact origins are disputed, primarily between Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal (where it is believed to have originated from the Wolof dish Thieboudienne). The flavor profile often includes a signature smoky taste achieved by allowing the rice to toast slightly at the bottom of the pot.

Afrobeats

Live Music Music Production Nightlife Ideas

A blanket term used to describe popular music from West Africa and the diaspora that initially developed in Nigeria, Ghana, and the UK in the 2000s and 2010s.

It is distinct from Afrobeat (without the 's'), a genre pioneered by Fela Kuti in the 1970s. Modern Afrobeats blends highlife, hiplife, jùjú music, and local rhythms with contemporary R&B, hip hop, and dancehall.

Négritude

Literature Ideas Visual Art

A literary and ideological movement developed by francophone black intellectuals, writers, and politicians in France during the 1930s.

Its founders united in their rejection of French colonial racism and domination. The movement aimed to cultivate "Black consciousness" across Africa and its diaspora, celebrating African heritage and values.

CONCEPT

Ubuntu

Ideas Tech Culture Literature

A Nguni Bantu term meaning "humanity". It is often translated as "I am because we are", or "humanity towards others".

In Southern Africa, it has come to be used as a term for a kind of humanist philosophy, ethic, or ideology, also known as Ubuntuism propagated in the Africanisation process of these countries during the 1980s and 1990s.

"Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu"

FASHION

Agbada

Fashion & Streetwear Visual Art Ideas

A four-piece male attire found among the Yoruba of southwestern Nigeria and the Republic of Benin. It consists of a large, free-flowing outer robe (awosoke), an undervest (awotele), a pair of long trousers (sokoto), and a hat (fìla).

The agbada is a prominent symbol of status and wealth. It is typically worn for formal events, weddings, and traditional ceremonies. Modern interpretations have seen it adapted into womenswear and contemporary high fashion.
