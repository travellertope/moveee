About this journey

Lagos is not a destination.
It's a conversation.

This is not a tour. There is no coach, no laminated itinerary, no group photo at Eko Hotel. What we offer is five days of access — to artists, makers, chefs, musicians, curators, and the one painter in Yaba who has refused every gallery outside Nigeria and is about to show you why.

We move in the hours Lagos saves for itself: early mornings in Balogun market before the crowd, late afternoons in studios, supper tables that go past midnight. The city is the curriculum.

The anchor of this journey is the opening of Mother Tongue — Ifeoma Okoli's first solo exhibition at Kó Gallery. On the night of 19 April, you will be in the room when each sitter sees her portrait for the first time.

Before you ask

Common questions.

Is this suitable if I've never been to Lagos? +
Yes — and in some ways, ideal. You arrive without preconceptions. We brief you thoroughly beforehand and our hosts are with you throughout. About half of our travellers on past journeys had never visited the city before.
What's the group dynamic like? +
We keep it at twelve for a reason. Past groups have included artists, writers, architects, a chef, a VP at a tech company, and a retired teacher — all brought together by the cultural anchor. Nobody has ever reported feeling out of place.
Do I need a visa? +
Nigerian passport holders: no. UK, US, Canadian, and most EU passport holders require a Nigerian visa on arrival or an e-visa, which we walk you through in the pre-departure pack. Allow 2 weeks. Contact us if you need specific guidance.
Can I join just for the opening night? +
The opening night (19 April) is separately RSVPable via the Mother Tongue event page. The studio visit and supper table on Day 3 are exclusive to Origins journey travellers and are not available individually.
What's the accommodation situation? +
We don't book accommodation on your behalf, but we provide a curated list at three price points — from a boutique guesthouse on the mainland to apartments in Ikoyi. Most travellers stay on the island for ease of movement.
Is the Ifeoma studio visit definitely happening? +
Yes. This is confirmed and is the centrepiece of the itinerary — not a "subject to availability" afterthought. It is why this journey exists. In the unlikely event of a change, we would restructure the programme and communicate immediately.
Is the price the same for Nigerian travellers? +
We offer naira pricing (₦580,000 / Connect: ₦520,000) for Nigerian residents and sterling pricing (£320 / Connect: £290) for diaspora and international travellers. We don't believe in a single dollar price for a journey that starts in Lagos.
What happens if I need to cancel? +
Full refund of deposit if you cancel within 7 days of booking. 50% refund if you cancel 21+ days before departure. No refund within 21 days (but we can transfer your spot to someone else with our help). If we cancel: full refund, always.
